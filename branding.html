Branding

How we're getting our brand to you

And why it's important


As the only business of its kind currently in Vancouver, we will require vigorous branding to get our name out there in the first year to attract subscribers. World of BeerCraft will be advertised across various mediums to get our company name known to interested parties.


In the year before World of BeerCraft opens, people around Vancouver will notice two large billboards advertising the service. These billboard ads will cycle across the city over the period of a year, helping to establish an initial interest in our company.


To help grow our initial subscriber base, we plan to begin door to door advertising to help get our name out there. Coupled with current subscribers spreading the company by word of mouth is expected to generate interest in our service.


Facebook advertising is another medium being used. Using the marketing tool available, we found that we can target advertisements to Facebook users who are 20 years or older in the Vancouver area. The system estimates 5-28 people will like the advertisement per day, and the ad will run year-round. Total price for this venture is simply $7 per day, providing a cheap means of reaching a large volume of potential customers.


Another method to promote our business is using our logo. Taking homage from popular MMORPG games, which many people know, we can use this distinction to our advantage. As it shares a similar style, this logo is expected to stand out for most people, increasing the chance they will read and investigate our company further. We plan to include our logo at partnered breweries so customers who normally buy their brews from the brewery directly will take note of our business and potentially subscribe. Additionally, our delivery vehicles will be outfitted with decals advertising World of BeerCraft to draw interest as they are driving around Vancouver making deliveries.


Due to our good will efforts in donating to Alcoholics Anonymous, we believe that our public image will be quite favourable in Vancouver. In doing so, we brand ourselves as a socially responsible business that does not care for only their customer base but society as a whole.
