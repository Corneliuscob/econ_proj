Ethics

What we stand for as a company

Our values and culture


At World of BeerCraft, not only do we make the process of getting beer to your home hassle free, we also personalize it to your tastes. For some, going to the liquor store may be out of their way, or they do not want to deal with the recyclables afterwards. We take care of all that. Furthermore, this is because we believe in adequate compensation for all personnel. Given the high cost of living in the Vancouver area, we strive to pay our workers a fair wage. Additionally, no gratuity is requried in our business model whereas similar delivery businesses encourage it. As a premium subscription, we believe that the company should pay a fair wage so subscribers are not obligated to tip. Please note that unlike other delivery companies such as Uber Eats or SkipTheDishes, we do not require employees to use their own vehicles. World of BeerCraft maintains their own fleet of delivery cars to help reduce economic strain on workers. As a socially responsible company our prices are relfective of the points above.


World of BeerCraft takes pride in its customer base and strives to provide a convenient service for all subscribers. To ensure the company is well within the bounds of the law, stringent requirements are required with regards to identification. By law, the legal drinking age in British Columbia is 19 years of age. To guarantee all subscribers are of age, we require credit card information as well as a scanned piece of photo ID. Additionally, upon initial delivery, our delivery personnel will ask to see the card and ID used to sign up to verify your identity. Signatures are required before the delivery is completed to ensure complete consent of the client. We apologize for any inconveniences in this process, but we strongly hold to our professional standards and do not wish to service minors.


Understanding the risks of alcohol abuse, we at World of BeerCraft hope that our clients will always drink responsibly. As such, $3/mo from every subscription is donated to Alcoholics Anonymous to help those struggling with alcoholism. As a service that sells alcohol, we respect the decision of those who prefer sobriety, and want to show our support for those who make the choice to do so.
