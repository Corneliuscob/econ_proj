Ethics

What we stand for as a company

Our values and culture


World of BeerCraft takes pride in its customer base and strives to provide a convenient service for all subscribers. To ensure the company is well within the bounds of the law, stringent requirements are required with regards to identification. By law, the legal drinking age in British Columbia is 19 years of age. To guarantee all subscribers are of age, we require credit card information as well as a scanned piece of photo ID. Additionally, upon initial delivery, our delivery personnel will ask to see the card and ID used to sign up to verify your identity. Signatures are required before the delivery is completed to ensure complete consent of the client. We apologize for any inconveniences in this process, but we strongly hold to our professional standards and do not wish to service minors.


Some people may question the price for this service, when they could go buy craft beers on their own for cheaper or have it delivered using a delivery service. At World of BeerCraft, not only do we make the process of getting beer to your home hassle free, we also personalize it to your tastes. For some, going to the liquor store may be out of their way, or they do not want to deal with the recyclables afterwards. We take care of all that. Furthermore, this is because we believe in adequate compensation for all personnel. Given the high cost of living in the Vancouver area, we strive to pay our workers a fair wage. Additionally, we do not believe in gratuity in our business model. Whereas similar delivery businesses may encourage tipping your delivery driver, we in fact discourage it. As a premium subscription, we believe that the company should pay a fair wage without the need for tips, hence the price. Please note that unlike other delivery companies such as Uber Eats or SkipTheDishes, we do not require employees to use their own vehicles. World of BeerCraft maintains their own fleet of delivery cars to help reduce economic strain on workers.
